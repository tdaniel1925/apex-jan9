# Insurance Jargon Elimination Dictionary

**Purpose**: Translate industry jargon into plain language that both experienced agents and newcomers can understand.

---

## Core Terminology

| **Jargon Term** | **Plain English** | **Context/Usage** |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **IUL** (Indexed Universal Life) | Cash Value Life Insurance | "Permanent life insurance that builds cash value" |
| **FYC** (First Year Commission) | First-Year Earnings | "What you earn on a new policy in year one" |
| **IMO/MGA** (Independent Marketing Org) | Insurance Agency | "The organization that supports agents" |
| **Captive Agent** | Exclusive Agent | "An agent who can only sell one company's products" |
| **Street-level contracts** | Top-tier commission rates | "The highest commission levels available in the industry" |
| **Vested / Vesting schedule** | When it becomes yours | "Your commissions and client relationships belong to you immediately" |
| **Book** (agent's book) | Client base / Portfolio | "The clients you work with and the ongoing income they provide" |
| **Renewals** | Ongoing commissions | "Income you continue to earn year after year from your clients" |
| **Clawback** | Taking back commissions | "When a company takes back money you already earned if a client cancels" |
| **Overrides** | Team bonuses | "Extra income you earn when agents you mentor make sales" |
| **Production** | Sales volume | "The amount of business you write" |
| **Contracting** | Getting authorized | "The paperwork process to represent insurance companies" |
| **Appointment** | Authorization | "Official permission to sell a carrier's products" |
| **Carrier** | Insurance Company | "The actual insurance companies (like State Farm, AIG, etc.)" |
| **A-Rated** | Top-rated | "Insurance companies with excellent financial strength ratings" |
| **Commission split** | Shared commission | "When you have to split your earnings with your agency" |
| **Quotas** | Minimum sales requirements | "Required sales targets you must hit each month" |
| **Down-line** | Your team | "Agents you've recruited and mentor" |
| **Up-line** | Your sponsor | "The person who brought you into the business" |
| **Writing business** | Making sales | "Helping clients purchase insurance policies" |
| **Place a policy** | Complete a sale | "When a client signs up and the policy is issued" |

---

## Phrases to Avoid or Rewrite

### ❌ Jargon-Heavy
> "We offer street-level FYC contracts up to 145% with full vesting from day one, 6 levels of overrides, and no clawbacks on your book."

### ✅ Plain English
> "You earn top industry commissions (up to 145%) that belong to you immediately. Plus, you earn team bonuses when you help others succeed. No one takes back money you've already earned."

---

### ❌ Jargon-Heavy
> "Unlike captive shops, we're an independent IMO with appointments to 7 A-rated carriers."

### ✅ Plain English
> "Unlike agents who can only sell one company's products, you'll have access to 7 top-rated insurance companies. This means you can always find the right policy for your client."

---

### ❌ Jargon-Heavy
> "Fast contracting and appointment transfers for experienced producers with strong production history."

### ✅ Plain English
> "We'll get you authorized to sell within 48-72 hours. If you're currently with another agency, we'll help handle the transfer paperwork."

## Writing Style Guidelines

### 1. **Lead with the Benefit, Not the Feature**
- ❌ "145% FYC contracts"
- ✅ "Earn top commissions that let you build wealth faster"

### 2. **Use Active, Personal Language**
- ❌ "Agents are contracted with carriers"
- ✅ "We'll get you authorized to sell"

### 3. **Explain, Don't Assume Knowledge**
- ❌ "No vesting schedules"
- ✅ "Your clients and income are yours from day one—no waiting period"

### 4. **Show, Don't Tell**
- ❌ "High commission structure"
- ✅ "Many agents double their income in their first year"

### 5. **Replace Industry Terms with Everyday Words**
- ❌ "Build your down-line"
- ✅ "Grow your team"

---

## Quick Reference Checklist

Before publishing any copy, verify:

- [ ] No acronyms without explanation (IMO, FMO, FYC, IUL, etc.)
- [ ] "Carrier" replaced with "insurance company" for newcomers
- [ ] "Contracting" explained or replaced with "getting authorized"
- [ ] "Vesting" explained or replaced with "when it becomes yours"
- [ ] "Production" replaced with "sales" or "business"
- [ ] "Book" replaced with "client base" or "portfolio"
- [ ] Technical terms defined on first use
- [ ] Benefit-driven headlines, not feature-driven

---

**When in doubt**: If a 16-year-old wouldn't immediately understand the term, simplify it.
